An exercise in identifying and correcting run-on sentences

Part 1 – Find the run-on sentences
  1. Read the text carefully. Ten sentences are run-ons, meaning that two complete sentences have been incorrectly joined without proper punctuation or a connecting word.
  2. Hover over a sentence to highlight it. Click any sentence you believe is a run-on.
  3. Click a selected sentence again to deselect it.
  4. When you are ready, press Check answers. You will see which selections are wrong, but the key is not revealed.
  5. Correct any mistakes and check again – repeat until all ten are found.
